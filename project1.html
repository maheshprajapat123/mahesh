This paper discusses the effect of anisotropy in terms of anisotropic ratio on ductile materials subjected to mode-I or opening type of loading. Hill – von Mises’s yield criterion is used for modeling anisotropic stress triaxiality and plastic zone equations. The results are generated and were compared in the form of polar plot for plane strain condition at different Poisson’s ratio. In addition to this, the effect of stress triaxiality with respect to anisotropy ratio is also investigated. It is observed that anisotropic plane stress problem of fracture is almost equivalent to isotropic fracture in plane strain condition.
Keywords: Anisotropy, stress triaxiality, plastic zone, fracture mechanics, yield.
